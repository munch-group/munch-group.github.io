Jakob’s MAGMA pilot

In a pilot study of ASD GWAS iPsych GWAS summary statistics (MAGMA), separately analyzing males and females to avoid confounding sex-specific effects, we find the following positive associations with ASD risk:

  • Xi escape genes in males (p-value: 0.027, beta: 0.03 )
  • Gametologs in females (p-value: 0.026, beta: 0.45)
  • Neuron+spermatid genes in males (p-value: 0.030, beta: 0.14) (All neuron genes not)

NB: Note the strange observation that ECH regions seem to be “protective” against ASD:

  • non-PAR X genes (NPX) show no association, but when subtracting ECH-genes (NPX-OvHuSw), the association is almost significant (p-value: 0.059) with a beta of 0.17.

Other enrichments

Ari’s selection scan

  • Among brain-expressed genes, ASD genes are enriched for genes also expressed in spermatids (p-value: 0.0012). This property may just characterize ASD genes as pleiotropic ”motor genes,” but it may also result from an ascertainment bias of ASD genes if the tissue sharing and drive that has promoted variants are detected as ASD genes.

Shannon’s offspring ratio distortion

Significant SNPs across the 88-95Mb region roughly match the region translocated to Y in humans.

  • Is it driven by PCDH11X, TGIF2LX, or both?
  • It might be the entire region if the Y inversion (or a new similar one) is segregating.

The translocation would have introduced a lot of selective pressure on dosage compensation males. There would be strong selection to resolve this by evolving separate (possibly sex-antagonistic) functions or expressions for the translocated genes. The gametolog property may have allowed them to evolve into drivers. Most of the region later inverted on Y, truncating TGIF2LY, but this is still expressed. I am not sure anyone knows if the Y inversion could still be segregating in humans. If the inversion happened once, it might also be recurrent.

MSCI-Xi hypothesis

Male meiotic sex-chromosome inactivation (MSCI), which limits the scope for meiotic drive in spermatogenesis, and somatic X chromosome inactivation (XCI), which inactivates the extra X in females, most likely evolved from the same highly conserved mechanism of un-synapsed chromatin silencing (Hornecker et al. 2007) and thus overlap in their control and chromatin organization. I hypothesize that meiotic drivers, in addition to other detrimental effects, fuel an inactivation-dosage-conflict between the sexes: If genes that escape MSCI in males also escape XCI in females, the deleterious excess female dosage must be resolved by lower gene expression, in turn leading to dosage deficiency in males (Figure below). Jakob’s MAGMA pilot seems to comfort the hypothesis’ two predictions:

  1. ASD association in males is enriched among genes escaping female XCI.
  2. ASD association in females is enriched among gametologs (candidate meiotic driver genes with a homolog on the Y chromosome).

Hitchhiking hypothesis

ASD risk-variants “hitchhike” to a higher frequency with genes under strong selection or sperm function.

Chromatin structure hypothesis

Super-loops established by CTCF binding at the XIST, FIRRE, and DXZ4 loci are crucial to proper X inactivation. However, new results show that DXZ4 is also a speciation gene in cats responsible for infertility in hybrid males through its effect on MSCI. Genes with such dual roles in Xi and MSCI suggest that strong selection for genes to escape MSCI may affect or, in some cases, even determine whether the same genes escape Xi. Drivers at the edges of suppressed heterochromatin domains may be exposed if the X is paired with a Y with a repeat composition draining chromatin-maintaining genes. Drivers may also modify chromatin to control their expression by increasing ampliconic gene copy number or by modifying linked euchromatin-insulating CTFC tandem repeat motifs (Giorgetti et al. 2016).
