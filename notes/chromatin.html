Inversions on X

Felines have eight times as few inversions (>50kb) as primates, but felines X are enriched for inversions over autosomes. A 45mb coldspot region has a lot of fixed inversions, particularly fixed ones, most of which contain a protein-coding gene (Bredemeyer et al. 2023).

CTCF

Human DXZ4 is a tandem repeat array containing 56 monomers, where each repeat contains a single CTCF-binding site (Figueroa et al. 2015).

CTCFL, expressed before meiosis (strangely, on SpermEvol, this seems to be true for humans but not mice where expression peaks in round spermatids), has a similar function and, in mice, binds to a similar motif in a nucleosome composition-dependent manner with little overlap with CTCF binding sites (Sleutels et al. 2012). CTCFL in mice causes sub-fertility because of partially penetrant testicular atrophy (Sleutels et al. 2012).

DXZ4

In humans, the long noncoding RNAs, DANT1 and DANT, are expressed from DXZ4 on the inactive chrX (Xi), promoting superlooping (Figueroa et al. 2015). They also facilitate the localization of the Barr body to the nucleolar membrane in mammals (Dossin & Heard 2022).

DXZ4 is a major-effect hybrid male sterility locus in the Felis genus (Bredemeyer et al. 2021). Germ cells of sterile male hybrids show RA-specific methylation defects and DANT1 misregulation, resulting in failed MSCI and meiotic arrest, a phenotype also seen in mice hybrids (Larson et al. 2016). In cats, the DXZ4 macrosatelite has a RA and RB array (human is RA), each with repeats binding CTCF, and here, the RA is in the top 0.5% of the most rapidly evolving 5-kb windows across cats (Bredemeyer et al. 2023). Ablation (“tissue-specific knockout”) of DXZ4 does not change the silenced state of the inactive chrX in mice and humans (Bonora et al. 2018; Froberg et al. 2018). However, its conservation suggests that DXZ4 is important to XCI and maybe MSCI.

Analyzing borders of existing assemblies (Bredemeyer et al. 2023) finds DXZ4 across mammals with species having either the RA or RB array with relatively constrained repeat sizes (between 3 and 4.9 kb). Strangely, Bovids have lost DXZ4.
